Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about our fertility treatments and specialized services

What is IVF treatment and how successful is it?

In vitro fertilization (IVF) is a comprehensive fertility treatment where eggs are retrieved from the ovaries and fertilized with sperm in our laboratory. We offer personalized protocols with high success rates, carefully monitoring each step to maximize your chances of pregnancy.

How does IUI treatment differ from IVF?

Intrauterine insemination (IUI) is a less invasive procedure where prepared sperm is directly placed into the uterus during ovulation. We provide careful monitoring and precise timing to optimize the chances of fertilization, making it an excellent first-line treatment option.

What is egg freezing and who should consider it?

Egg freezing allows you to preserve your fertility potential for the future using advanced cryopreservation technology. This is ideal for women who want to delay childbearing for career, education, or personal reasons, or those facing medical treatments that may affect fertility.

What does comprehensive fertility testing include?

Our fertility testing includes comprehensive diagnostics to identify all factors affecting fertility. We conduct hormone assessments, imaging studies, genetic screening, and both male and female fertility evaluations to create a complete picture and personalized treatment plan.

How do your donor programs work?

Our donor programs feature carefully screened donors with comprehensive matching based on physical characteristics, medical history, and other important factors. We ensure all donors undergo rigorous medical and psychological screening to provide you with the best possible options.

What treatments are available for male and female infertility?

We offer specialized treatments for both partners, including pharmaceutical interventions, surgical options, and advanced assisted reproductive technologies. Our approach is tailored to address the specific cause of infertility, dramatically increasing the chances of successful pregnancy once diagnosed.
